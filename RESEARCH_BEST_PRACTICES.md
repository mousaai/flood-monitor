# Best Practices: Flood Water Management & Reuse
## Research Summary for FloodSat AI Region Detail Cards

### 1. Global Best Practices — Flood Water Reuse Technologies

#### A. Managed Aquifer Recharge (MAR) — الأكثر ملاءمة للإمارات
- **Description**: Directing floodwater into infiltration basins to recharge groundwater aquifers
- **UAE Application**: Used in Wadi Al Bih (Ras Al Khaimah), Wadi Ham (Fujairah)
- **Cost**: $0.10–0.50/m³ (cheapest option)
- **Efficiency**: Recovers 40–70% of stored water
- **Benefit-Cost Ratio**: 3:1 to 8:1
- **Sources**: MDPI Water 2022, ResearchGate UAE Groundwater Dams

#### B. Retention/Detention Basins — حوض التجميع
- **Description**: Surface storage ponds that hold floodwater for gradual release or reuse
- **Cost**: $55/m³ construction + $3.3/m³/year maintenance
- **Capacity**: Typically 0.5–5 million m³
- **Reuse**: Irrigation, dust suppression, construction water
- **UAE Example**: Al Ain detention basins, ADCD flood retention

#### C. Stormwater Harvesting for Irrigation
- **Description**: Collecting runoff for landscape/agricultural irrigation
- **Cost**: $0.20–0.80/m³ treated
- **Savings**: Replaces desalinated water at $1.5–3/m³
- **ROI**: 3–5 years
- **UAE**: ADSSC recycled water program, Abu Dhabi Al Ain Road project

#### D. Airport Stormwater Reuse (مطار أبوظبي)
- **Reference**: ICAO Water Management at Airports guide
- **Applications**:
  - Aircraft washing: 50–200 L/aircraft
  - Runway dust suppression: 10–50 m³/day
  - Landscape irrigation: 500–2000 m³/day
  - Construction water: 1000–5000 m³/event
  - Toilet flushing: 30–50% of building water demand
- **Cost of treatment**: $0.15–0.40/m³ (vs $2.50/m³ desalinated)
- **Annual savings**: AED 2–8 million/year for major airports

#### E. Green Infrastructure
- **Rain gardens**: Absorb 70–90% of runoff, cost $50–150/m²
- **Permeable pavements**: Infiltrate 100–300 mm/hr, cost $80–200/m²
- **Constructed wetlands**: Treat + store water, cost $100–300/m²
- **Green roofs**: Retain 50–80% rainfall, cost $150–400/m²

#### F. Flash Flood Spreading (تفريق الفيضانات)
- **Description**: Diverting wadi floods to spreading fields for infiltration
- **Iran/UAE experience**: 4.5 million m³/month recharge possible
- **Cost**: $0.05–0.15/m³ (cheapest of all)
- **UAE**: Al Dhahir, Al Ain wadis

### 2. Solution Scenarios by Region Type

#### Urban Dense Areas (أبوظبي المدينة، الشامخة)
- **Scenario A — Quick Win**: Smart drainage upgrade + IoT sensors
  - Cost: AED 5–15M per km²
  - Timeline: 6–18 months
  - Water recovery: 20–40%
- **Scenario B — Medium**: Underground detention tanks + reuse system
  - Cost: AED 20–50M per km²
  - Timeline: 2–4 years
  - Water recovery: 60–80%
- **Scenario C — Comprehensive**: Full green infrastructure retrofit
  - Cost: AED 80–200M per km²
  - Timeline: 5–10 years
  - Water recovery: 80–95%

#### Wadi/Rural Areas (الظاهر، العين)
- **Scenario A — Quick Win**: Earthen check dams + spreading fields
  - Cost: AED 1–5M per wadi
  - Timeline: 3–6 months
  - Recharge: 0.5–2M m³/year
- **Scenario B — Medium**: Concrete weirs + infiltration galleries
  - Cost: AED 10–30M per wadi
  - Timeline: 1–2 years
  - Recharge: 2–8M m³/year
- **Scenario C — Comprehensive**: MAR system + monitoring network
  - Cost: AED 30–100M per wadi
  - Timeline: 3–5 years
  - Recharge: 5–20M m³/year

#### Industrial/Airport Areas (المنطقة الصناعية، المطار)
- **Scenario A — Quick Win**: Stormwater collection tanks for non-potable reuse
  - Cost: AED 2–8M
  - Timeline: 6–12 months
  - Savings: AED 0.5–2M/year
- **Scenario B — Medium**: Treatment plant + distribution network
  - Cost: AED 15–40M
  - Timeline: 2–3 years
  - Savings: AED 3–8M/year
- **Scenario C — Comprehensive**: Zero-discharge system with full reuse
  - Cost: AED 50–150M
  - Timeline: 4–7 years
  - Savings: AED 8–20M/year

### 4. Prevention Strategies (تجنب تكرار الفيضانات)

1. **Early Warning System**: 72-hour advance warning reduces damage by 30–50%
2. **Land Use Planning**: Flood-plain zoning prevents 60–80% of future damage
3. **Drainage Network Upgrade**: Increases capacity from 10-year to 100-year events
4. **Nature-Based Solutions**: Wetlands, mangroves reduce peak flow by 20–40%
5. **Building Codes**: Flood-resistant construction reduces damage by 40–60%
